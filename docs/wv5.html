Weak Verbs III

Class III consists of four verbs: habban - to have, libban - to live, secgan - to say, and hycgan - to think/to intend. These verbs do not conjugate in the same way as each other, and can combine features of the first two weak classes, so they need to be learned individually. Since these verbs are very common, there are many variant spellings but the ones below are the most common.

  • Habban
  • Libban
  • Secgan
  • Hycgan

Depending on where or when a piece was written, words were often spelled differently. Class III weak verbs have many common variations, so you may see vowel mutation in the stem. For example: hygeþ, hycgeþ and hogaþ are all valid spellings of the 3rd person singular of hycgan. Similarly, the eo in libban is sometimes written as i. For example: lifaþ instead of leofaþ
